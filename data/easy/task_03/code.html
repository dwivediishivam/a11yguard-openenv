Scientists Discover New Method for Carbon Capture Using Ocean Algae

By James Rodriguez · Environmental Correspondent · April 2, 2025

Researchers at the Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution have announced a breakthrough in carbon capture technology that leverages the natural photosynthetic abilities of deep-sea algae.

The team, led by Dr. Maria Santos, found that a specific strain of diatom algae can absorb carbon dioxide at rates up to 20 times faster than terrestrial plants when cultivated under controlled conditions.

"This could be a game-changer for climate mitigation. We're essentially supercharging nature's own carbon removal system." — Dr. Maria Santos

The research, published in Nature Climate Change, outlines a scalable approach that could be deployed along coastlines worldwide within the next decade.

Early estimates suggest that a network of ocean-based algae farms could capture up to 2 gigatons of CO₂ annually, equivalent to roughly 5% of global emissions.
